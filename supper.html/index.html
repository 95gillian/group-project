Mseto wa Maharagwe (Rice with Beans) “Mseto wa Maharagwe” is the Swahili name for rice that is made with beans in one pot. It is a common recipe among many communities in Kenya. Mainly prepared as a main meal during lunch time, it is eaten by the whole family. Sometimes, this dish is eaten for supper.


Ingredients

  • 3 cups (505 g) kidney beans raw
  • 21 ¼ cups (4600 g) water
  • 4 cups (684 g) raw white rice, long grain
  • 1 large onion, red skinned, raw, unpeeled (206 g)
  • 2 tomatoes, red, ripe, raw (226 g)
  • 1 ¼ cups (238 g) cooking oil
  • 2 tbsp. (27 g) salt
